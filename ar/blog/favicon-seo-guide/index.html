Favicon SEO Guide

Google SERP snippets display favicons next to site names. Learn how to optimize yours to gain organic search clicks.

Favicons are no longer just aesthetic tab placeholders. In today's organic search landscape, they serve as prominent brand identifiers directly inside search engine results pages (SERPs).

Why Favicons Matter for SEO

While Google doesn't rank your site higher simply because you have a favicon, the visual icon directly affects search user behavior. A professional, clear, and high-contrast icon next to your page title increases brand credibility and visual relevance, driving higher click-through rates (CTR). Higher organic CTR signals page quality to search algorithms, indirectly boosting search ranking placement.

Google's Guidelines for Favicons

  • Be Representative: The icon must look like your brand logo or mark. Generic or misleading icons may be replaced with a default globe icon.
  • Crawlable URL: Ensure that the path to your icon is not blocked by your robots.txt file, especially for the Googlebot-Image crawler.
  • Square Ratio: E.g., 48x48px, 96x96px, or 192x192px. Google automatically scales the icon down to 16x16px for search results.
